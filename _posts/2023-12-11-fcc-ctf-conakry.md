> *Competing across borders with Redteam TG, at one of West Africa's leading cybersecurity forums.*

## The FCC

The **Forum de Conakry sur la Cybersécurité (FCC)** is one of West Africa's most significant cybersecurity events. Organized by **Guinea Cybersecurity (GCS)** and **ANSSI Guinée** (Guinea's National Cybersecurity Agency), it brings together government officials, cybersecurity experts, academic institutions, and private sector leaders to discuss cybersecurity challenges, critical infrastructure protection, and the future of digital security in the sub-region.

The 2023 edition was held on **December 11-12, 2023** at the Primus Hotel Kaloum in Conakry, Guinea, under the theme: *"Cybersecurity: The Central Role of Humans."*

The event includes a CTF competition as one of its flagship activities.

## Playing with Redteam TG

For this competition, I played alongside **Redteam TG**, a team that brings together some of the most skilled cybersecurity practitioners in Togo. Competing with them was both a challenge and an opportunity: the bar was high, the communication was efficient, and the strategy was sharp.

## The Result

The CTF ran in two phases:

| Phase | Result |
|---|---|
| Pre-qualification | **1st place** |
| Final | **3rd place** |

We dominated the pre-qualification stage, finishing first. In the final, we held a strong position and secured **3rd place** overall.

## Context

Crossing into Guinean territory for a competition, playing under a different flag with a cross-border team, and achieving a podium finish at a forum of this caliber was a meaningful milestone. It showed that the level I had built in Togo was competitive beyond national borders, and it reinforced the collaborative approach that defines how I approach cybersecurity: not just as an individual sport, but as a team discipline.
