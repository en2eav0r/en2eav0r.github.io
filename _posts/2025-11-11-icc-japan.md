## What is the ICC?

The **International Cybersecurity Challenge (ICC)** is the most prestigious and selective youth cybersecurity competition in the world. Created by a global consortium including ENISA (EU), Code Blue (Japan), Katzcy (USA), and NUST (Africa) — it brings together the top young talent from every continent.

Getting there is not a matter of signing up. Candidates are selected through a rigorous national and regional process based on performance records, individual skill level, CTF specialties, teamwork ability, and continuous competitive engagement throughout the year.

## The AFRICC Team

I competed as part of **AFRICC** — the African Regional Cybersecurity Committee — making its **first onsite appearance** at the ICC in Tokyo.

The team comprised **17 members representing 10+ African countries**, including Tunisia, Morocco, Namibia, Uganda, Nigeria, South Africa, Zimbabwe, and Togo.

## What This Experience Meant

Being selected for the ICC placed me officially among the **continental elite of Africa in cybersecurity**, under the age of 26.

> *"If you want to be the best, you need to face the best, learn from them, push yourself to their level, and eventually surpass them."*

When I returned, I designed the IPNET CyberSec Club's methodology around that principle. Two members of the club I built were subsequently selected for the ICC themselves — following the same path.
