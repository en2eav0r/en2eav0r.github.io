## What is the ICC?

The **International Cybersecurity Challenge (ICC)** is a global youth cybersecurity competition created by a consortium including ENISA (EU), Code Blue (Japan), Katzcy (USA), and NUST (Africa). One team per continent. The top young players from each region, selected through a rigorous national and regional process based on performance records, CTF specialties, and competitive track record throughout the year.

You don't sign up. You get picked.

## The AFRICC team

I competed as part of **AFRICC**, the African Regional Cybersecurity Committee, making its **first onsite appearance** at the ICC in Tokyo.

The team had **17 members from 10+ African countries**, including Tunisia, Morocco, Namibia, Uganda, Nigeria, South Africa, Zimbabwe, and Togo.

## What this meant

Getting selected for the ICC put me among the top young cybersecurity players on the African continent, under 26.

> *"If you want to be the best, you need to face the best, learn from them, push yourself to their level, and eventually surpass them."*

When I came back, I redesigned the IPNET CyberSec Club's methodology around that principle. Two members of the club were subsequently selected for the ICC themselves, following the same path.
