> *2nd in Togo. 76th in Africa. A team of four second-year students, competing against the continent.*

## The competition

The **ECOWAS Hackathon** is a cybersecurity hacking competition organized by the Economic Community of West African States (CEDEAO). It runs with a national bracket first, then an African-wide ranking.

## The result

<img src="/assets/images/ecowas/ecowas-2nd-place-poster.jpg" alt="IPNET official ECOWAS Hackathon 2022 poster" style="border-radius: 10px; width: 65%;" />

A team of four second-year Licence Cybersecurity students from IPNET:

- **2nd place nationally**, out of **24 Togolese teams**
- **76th place in Africa**, out of **119 African teams**

The team that finished 1st went on to represent Togo at the continental final.

## The lesson

76th out of 119 African teams in our second year. Respectable — but 2nd nationally means we were one position away from the only slot that matters.

That gap stayed with me. The hunger to not fall one place short drove everything that came after: the preparation, the CTF club, and eventually the national title.
